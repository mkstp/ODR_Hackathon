# Presentation Brief: MinorSafe

**Purpose:** persuade
**Audience:** Hackathon judges — expected to have general tech and legal literacy; may not be familiar with ODR or AI guardrail systems
**Key takeaway:** MinorSafe is a practical, working prototype that protects minors in AI chat interfaces by detecting harm patterns before they escalate — built with expert legal input on harm categories and expert computational design to make real-time AI safety viable at scale
**Scope:** ~10 slides, 3–4 sections, ~10 minutes
**Owner:** Marc St-Pierre

## Sources
- docs/project_charter.md
- README.md
- docs/reports/change_and_decision_log.jsonl

## Constraints
- Persuasive tone: this is a competition pitch, not a report
- Emphasize the ODR angle as a differentiator — MinorSafe is prevention-first, not content moderation
- Highlight expert team contributions: legal (harm categories, system prompt) and computational (prompt caching, sliding window context, session state injection, turn weighting)
- Keep slide content tight — 10 minutes means no slide should hold the room for more than 90 seconds
- Do not dwell on what is out of scope; frame out-of-scope items as the production roadmap
